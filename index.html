— Notes from the Curator

Walk in and you'll catch the view first — not your phone, not the espresso bar, but the room itself. Warm woods. Soft daylight. A piano somewhere in the speakers, playing slowly enough that you might catch yourself slowing down with it.

The menu is intentionally short. The Biscoff Einspänner has its own following. The Rosie Tonic, refreshing and mildly floral, is the spring favorite. Order one, sit by the window, and stay through three songs at least.

It's a soft opening, which means you'll catch the place at its quietest — before the city catches on.
